About the Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise (pronounced BEE-shawn FREE-say) is a charming, small dog breed known for its fluffy white coat and playful personality. These cheerful companions are beloved family pets around the world for their friendly nature and adaptable temperament.

Bichons typically stand 9-12 inches tall and weigh between 7-12 pounds, making them perfect apartment dogs with a moderate exercise need. Despite their small size, they have big personalities and boundless energy.

Characteristics

Temperament

  • Playful and energetic
  • Affectionate and loving
  • Social and friendly with strangers
  • Good with children and other pets
  • Intelligent and trainable
  • Rarely aggressive

Physical Traits

  • Powder-puff appearance with curly white coat
  • Dark, round eyes with an alert expression
  • Compact body with a plumed tail carried over the back
  • Minimal shedding (great for allergy sufferers)
  • Life expectancy of 14-15 years

Caring for Your Bichon

Grooming

Bichons require regular grooming to maintain their beautiful coat:

  • Daily brushing to prevent matting
  • Professional grooming every 4-6 weeks
  • Regular bathing with dog-friendly shampoo
  • Special attention to eye and ear cleaning

Exercise

While not high-energy dogs, Bichons need regular exercise:

  • Daily walks (20-30 minutes)
  • Interactive playtime
  • Mental stimulation through training and toys

Health Considerations

Bichons are generally healthy but can be prone to:

  • Allergies and skin issues
  • Ear infections
  • Patellar luxation
  • Dental problems

History

The Bichon Frise has a rich history dating back centuries:

  • Descended from Mediterranean water spaniels
  • Became popular with European nobility during the Renaissance
  • Favored by Spanish and French royal courts
  • Served as sailing companions on trade ships
  • Recognized as an official breed by the American Kennel Club in 1972
